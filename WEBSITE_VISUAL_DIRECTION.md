# Colattao — Website Visual Direction

A premium visual direction for a future Colattao website concept
(`/website-concept`). The current site at colattao.com already has the
essentials. **This concept adds brand depth, interaction, and cultural
identity** — it builds on what's approved rather than replacing it.

Design north star: a **living brand ecosystem**, not a template — premium
Colombian coffeehouse, single-origin story, boho warmth, local artwork,
Virginia Beach community, and Colombian cultural texture.

Palette anchors: **espresso brown · parchment · warm gold · ceramic blue**,
nostalgic but modern.

---

## 1. What the current website already does well

The approved site has a real foundation we should preserve and amplify:

- **Strong taglines with soul** — "Pure Origin. Singular Taste.", "Where
  Every Cup Has a Story." These already carry the single-origin narrative.
- **Clear value proposition** — "100% single origin coffee" and "Your Spot
  for Coffee, Eats, and Everything In-Between" set expectations well.
- **Complete functional essentials** — reservations, catering, party/group
  reservations, jobs / apply now. The site already *works* as a business tool.
- **Community signals** — Google reviews, story/about section, gallery,
  Instagram + Google links. Social proof and personality are present.
- **Practical info** — location, hours, contact are all covered.
- **Identity cues** — boho-inspired space, homemade pastries, local artwork,
  community feel. The brand already knows who it is.

**Takeaway:** the content and intent are right. The opportunity is in
*visual hierarchy, cultural texture, and interaction* — not in rewriting what
Colattao stands for.

---

## 2. What the current website is missing visually

Framed as additive opportunities, not criticisms:

- **A signature visual rhythm.** Sections could share one consistent
  golden-ratio cadence so the eye flows instead of resets.
- **Colombian cultural texture as design language.** Mochila weave, sombrero
  vueltiao silhouettes, ceramic-blue floral motifs — used as refined accents,
  not stock decoration.
- **Depth and warmth in imagery.** Editorial, story-driven photography with
  consistent grade (espresso shadows, parchment highlights, gold warmth).
- **A "single-origin story" moment.** A dedicated, visually rich origin
  narrative — bean to Virginia Beach cup.
- **Interaction as brand.** The Café Rush game, digital menu, and stickers
  give Colattao something most coffeehouse sites don't have — a reason to
  return and share.
- **Typographic hierarchy.** Elegant serif display (Playfair) paired with a
  clean sans (Inter) to separate brand voice from utility text.
- **Cohesive button & card system.** One premium component language across
  reservations, catering, menu, and apply-now CTAs.

---

## 3. Golden-ratio layout plan

Use the golden ratio (≈ 61.8% / 38.2%) consistently so every screen feels
composed rather than stacked.

- **Hero split (mobile):** top 38.2% = brand zone (logo, tagline, primary
  CTA); bottom 61.8% = hero image / story imagery.
- **Hero split (desktop):** left 61.8% = editorial image; right 38.2% =
  wordmark + "Pure Origin. Singular Taste." + reserve / menu CTAs.
- **Section blocks:** each major section uses a 61.8/38.2 content-to-media (or
  media-to-content) split, alternating sides down the page for rhythm.
- **Type scale:** derive sizes from a 1.618 ratio (e.g. body 16 → subhead ~26
  → section title ~42 → hero ~68) for harmonious hierarchy.
- **Spacing:** vertical section padding and card gaps follow the same ratio
  (e.g. 24 / 38 / 62) so whitespace feels intentional.
- **Card grids:** gallery and menu cards sized so image-to-caption respects
  the golden split.

## 5. Section-by-section visual direction

1. **Hero** — Golden-ratio split. Wordmark + "Pure Origin. Singular Taste."
   over a warm pour/interior image. Primary CTAs: *Reserve a table*, *View
   menu*. Subtle gold glow + mochila corner accent.
2. **Where Every Cup Has a Story** — Editorial single-origin narrative band;
   alternating image/text blocks (bean → roast → Virginia Beach cup) with
   ceramic-blue rule dividers.
3. **Menu preview** — Parchment cards (reuse the existing digital menu look),
   a few signature drinks + "View full menu" link to `/menu` and the QR sign.
4. **La Cocina / Eats** — Food photography grid, "Your Spot for Coffee, Eats,
   and Everything In-Between" as the section voice.
5. **Reservations & events** — Three premium cards: *Table reservation*,
   *Catering*, *Party / group reservations*, each with an icon and CTA.
6. **The space / boho + local artwork** — Full-bleed interior imagery,
   community framing, gallery teaser.
7. **Community & reviews** — Google reviews styled as parchment quote cards;
   Instagram strip.
8. **Play / brand interaction** — Feature Café Rush + stickers: "Play while
   you wait" → links to `/` and `/owner-presentation`. This is the
   differentiator.
9. **Visit us** — Location, hours, contact, map; jobs / *Apply now* CTA.
10. **Footer** — Wordmark, social links, mochila hairline, copyright.

## 7. Copy tone

- **Warm, rooted, confident.** Speaks to origin, craft, and community.
- **Bilingual-friendly.** Spanish phrases as natural texture ("cafecito",
  "la cocina", "pura cepa") where it adds soul, never as gimmick.
- **Story-first.** Lead with narrative ("Where Every Cup Has a Story"), follow
  with utility.
- **Invitational, not salesy.** "Reserve a table", "Pull up a chair",
  "Come for the coffee, stay for the people."
- **Premium but unpretentious.** Editorial, not corporate. Boho, not stiff.

---

## 8. What to avoid

- ❌ Mentioning competitor / chain names in visible site copy.
- ❌ Insulting or visibly "replacing" the current site — this is an evolution.
- ❌ Generic stock-photo coffee clichés with no cultural specificity.
- ❌ Overusing Colombian motifs to the point of caricature — refined accents
  only.
- ❌ Cluttered layouts; respect the golden-ratio whitespace.
- ❌ Mixing too many fonts — stick to Playfair (display) + Inter (body).
- ❌ Flat, gradeless imagery that breaks the espresso/parchment/gold mood.
- ❌ Heavy JS or anything that compromises the fast, static, mobile-first feel.

---

## 9. Implementation order for `/website-concept`

A safe, incremental build sequence (each step independently buildable):

1. **Scaffold the route** `/website-concept` as a static page using the
   existing Colattao design system (globals.css + theme config).
2. **Hero section** — golden-ratio split, wordmark, tagline, primary CTAs.
3. **Story band** — "Where Every Cup Has a Story" single-origin narrative.
4. **Menu preview** — reuse parchment cards; link to `/menu`.
5. **Reservations / catering / events** — three premium cards.
6. **Space + community + reviews** — imagery + parchment quote cards.
7. **Play / interaction feature** — Café Rush + stickers, links to `/` and
   `/owner-presentation`.
8. **Visit us + apply now** — location/hours/contact + jobs CTA.
9. **Footer** — wordmark, social, mochila hairline.
10. **Polish pass** — cultural texture accents, dividers, consistent grade,
    accessibility + performance check.

> Each step should keep `/website-concept` static and mobile-first, reuse
> `appTheme` and the existing component language, and never touch gameplay,
> EventBus, GameCanvas, or analytics.
